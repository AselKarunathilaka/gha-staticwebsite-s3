The Mesozoic Era

A Journey Through the History of Dinosaurs

The Dawn of the Giants

Dinosaurs roamed the Earth during the Mesozoic Era, which began about 252 million years ago and ended 66 million years ago. This era is divided into three major periods: the Triassic, Jurassic, and Cretaceous. During this immense span of time, dinosaurs evolved into a spectacular array of shapes and sizes, dominating the terrestrial ecosystems.

The Jurassic Period

The Jurassic Period (201 to 145 million years ago) saw the supercontinent Pangaea begin to split apart. This period is famous for massive sauropods like the Brachiosaurus and Diplodocus, which fed on towering conifers. Iconic armored dinosaurs also emerged during this time, defending themselves against fierce predators.

The Cretaceous Period

Spanning from 145 to 66 million years ago, the Cretaceous Period was the final and longest segment of the Mesozoic Era. It witnessed the highest diversity of dinosaurs. It was also during this period that the first flowering plants appeared, forever changing the Earth's landscape.

This magnificent era came to an abrupt end when a massive asteroid struck the Yucatán Peninsula, triggering a mass extinction event that wiped out all non-avian dinosaurs.
